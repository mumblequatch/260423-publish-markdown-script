SOLDIER #1

(Castilian Spanish)

Come on, he's getting away!

SOLDIER #2

They don't pay me enough for this!

EXT. CITY OF DIS, ALLEY — NIGHT

Then a change in atmosphere — less open. An alley.

The footsteps skid to a stop.

Child breathing heavily.

Blood pounding in our ears.

Nearby, a pair of boots splash to a stop.

SOLDIER #1

Wait, he went in that alley!

SOLDIER #2

(further away)

No, the street! The street!

SOLDIER #1

I swear I saw…

SOLDIER #2

Forget the alley! Let's go!

Both pairs of bootsteps recede.

Leaving just the breathing.

And the rain.

Then the CREAK of an old, wooden door.

A sharp intake of breath:

BENJAMIN

I'm sorry, I was just…

BERTHOLD

(tired, but warm, Cockney accent)

Don't worry, I ain't gonna scold away a lonely child on a rainy night.

(beat)

Whatcha doing out here, anyway?

(beat)

Who's after you?

BENJAMIN

I don't know. Soldiers or something.

BERTHOLD

Lord Volando's men? Didn't know they'd sunk to terrorizing children. Why're they after you?

BENJAMIN

I… I don't know.

BERTHOLD

Well… hell, whyncha come inside for a bit. Can't have you out here soaked and drippin' and all by your lonesome, can we?

BENJAMIN

Are you… with them?

BERTHOLD

With them!

(chuckles)

Good god, no. Nobody's with them but them. Rest of us are just trying to get along.

(beat)

Come on in, hey? We'll just get you dried off and comfortable while they chase their tails a bit. Then we'll send you on your way.

BENJAMIN

Ok… okay.

Footsteps in a puddle.

BERTHOLD

That's right. Just a few ticks indoors, bum in a seat, will do wonders. Might even be able to…

Hang on, stop. Don't turn away, now. Come on, face toward the light.

Rain.

Berthold — a sharp intake of breath.

BERTHOLD

(flat, empty)

I'm sorry, son. I can't help you.

What's wrong?

Go on, get out of here.

But I don't…

What happened?

VOICE #2

You know, the boy!

VOICES

(calling)

Running footsteps, jostling pack, splashing.

EXT. CITY OF DIS, PORT — NIGHT

Finally, the bells are quiet.

A different sound has replaced them: the OCEAN. The CLINK of rigging, the CREAK of wooden boats.

Footsteps slowing, breathing. Footsteps stop.

Blood pounding in ears.

Breathing slows.

NARRATOR

Breathless and exhausted beyond fear, Benjamin finally emerged from the labyrinthine streets of the city to find himself in a port.

He knew he wasn't safe, but he was finally free of the prying eyes of the drooping, close-packed tenements.

Here were only the blind walls of crumbling warehouses. And a glowing, grey-green sea that filled a bay and then stretched like a luminous bubble to the horizon. Pale shapes moved beneath its surface, slow and distant, like ghosts of submarines.

The giant, black forms looming along the wharf reached their skeletal masts and spars into the starless sky, the rigging like threadbare shawls about their bony arms. Lights twinkled here and there from belowdecks, but otherwise the port was dead. Benjamin chuckled at the thought: it sure was.

That was when he heard the music. A guitar, plucked slowly. And a deep voice singing. Benjamin's heart sank — he wasn't alone after all.

But the melody was simple, and sad and his tired mind empty of other, immediate purpose, Benjamin followed it to the end of the wharf, and ducked behind a pyramid of moldering barrels to observe.

The music came from a shadowy figure sitting on a great coil of rope. From where he stood, Benjamin could see only that the figure wore a deep, black cloak that revealed a large, faintly-green nose and two pale, oversized hands that moved like graceful, white spiders over the strings.

A raven sat hunched nearby on a wooden post, its head cocked in Benjamin's direction, one glittering eye seeming to clock him where he hid.

The music stopped.

HOODED FIGURE

Don't be afraid. Come out. I shall not raise an alarm.

(beat)

I am not a coward, like my countrymen. Or at least not the same kind. Come here, boy. Come.

Footsteps.

HOODED FIGURE

So the rumors are true.

BENJAMIN

How do people even know I'm here?

HOODED FIGURE

The news of a living boy in the land of the dead travels fast.

(beat)

I never thought I should see a living soul again. It is almost too much for these old eyes to bear.

NARRATOR

The figure set the guitar aside.

HOODED FIGURE

It is a wonder you have evaded capture for so long. A wonder you are here at all.

(beat)

You must have a very important reason.

NARRATOR

He paused, as though listening to something Benjamin could not hear.

HOODED FIGURE

Now I understand. Your parents. You are looking for your parents.

BENJAMIN

You know where they are?

HOODED FIGURE

I know how to find out.

NARRATOR

One pale hand gestured toward the sea. Out in the gloom, a ship lay at anchor — black hull, black sails, lanterns burning with a sickly green fire.

We hear distant, drunken singing wafting across the water from the boat. Pirates.

HOODED FIGURE

That is the Naglfar. Her captain is Bartholomew Roberts. He and his crew possess something that does not belong to them.

BENJAMIN

What is it?

HOODED FIGURE

A very particular stone. It allows a skilled practitioner to see almost anywhere in the gray country — no matter how near or how far. It belongs to a creature of the deep, a demon old as the world. As long as it had this stone, it slept. It remained at peace. The pirates know this, but they took it anyway. They don't believe.

(beat)

But disbelief doesn't make a thing untrue.

BENJAMIN

Who are you, anyway?

HOODED FIGURE

I would tell you, but you would not live long enough to hear the answer.

BENJAMIN

So if you had the stone you could tell me where my parents are?

NARRATOR

The figure inclined his head.

BENJAMIN

Why haven't you taken it back yourself?

The raven laughs.

HOODED FIGURE

(somehow acknowledging the insult)

I was like you, long ago. Fearless and strong. I am no longer the author of history, merely its subject. But your story has only just begun, young one. Living one.

Bring me the stone.

EXT. UNDERWORLD, SPECTRAL OCEAN — NIGHT

NARRATOR

Benjamin found a boat — little more than a raft fitted with oars — and pushed out across the spectral water. It was warm and thick, more like oil than sea, glowing faintly beneath the surface. He did his best not to imagine what might be moving below.

The Naglfar loomed larger as he drew near.

Pirates singing.

NARRATOR

A great galleon, ancient and barnacled, its sails hanging limp in the windless air. And then he saw the hull.

It was studded with fingernails — thousands of them, yellowed and curling, pressed into the wood like scales.

SFX: pirates stop singing.

BENJAMIN

Benjamin grabbed the anchor chain and began to climb.

EXT. UNDERWORLD, PIRATE SHIP — CONTINUOUS

NARRATOR

It was not until he was actually pulling himself over the railing that he noticed the singing had stopped. What he saw then startled him so badly that he almost fell backward off the boat. He pinwheeled his arms to regain his balance and finally, barely, tucked a finger around the rail and pulled himself back aboard.

And then confronted again the bizarre tableau before him.

Skeletons dressed as pirates. Or, half-skeletons. Most had some amount of flesh still clinging to the bones. Half-faces. Grey men standing on legs of bones. And everything in between.

Standing, sitting, mugs of all description — some not even mugs — in their moldering fists. For some reason it jumped out at him: the source of the music. One skeleton with shiny, bright eyes situated in an otherwise bleached-white skull held an accordion. Next to him, a wooden mannequin's head sat atop an other wise complete, if deathly grey, body, stripped to the waist, which held a fiddle.

Every eye or pair of eyes or empty sockets stared at Benjamin.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

A round of applause, gentlemen, for our agile young alpinist — the rope-running rapscallion — the peerless, pocket-sized paladin — the plucky, palpitating, prodigiously…

(a beat as he flails, then with a flourish)

…preposterously pertinacious pilgrim of the pellucid provinces!

Silence on deck. Then, from near the mainmast: the rustle of old pages turning. A bony finger dragging down a column. A skeletal jaw clicking softly as it tests the words. A long pause.

SKELETON LIBRARIAN

(flat, bookish, unhurried)

Yep. Checks out.

The deck erupts. Bone mugs slam the rails. The accordion staggers back to life. The fiddle saws into a jig. The dead cheer — a dry, rattling sound, like wind through a boneyard.

NARRATOR

The Captain, for that's who he was, swept off his hat and bowed low — once to port, once to starboard.

Bartholomew Roberts was a big man — or had been, when he was alive. He wore a long coat of faded red velvet and a hat with a bedraggled feather. His beard was thick and black and tangled with what might have been seaweed or something worse. But the thing Benjamin noticed was his legs: two peg legs, both of them, carved from dark wood and clomping heavily on the deck as he walked. He turned to Benjamin and bowed again.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

My name is Captain Bartholomew Roberts and this is my ship, the Naglfar. And this assemblage of scurrilous scalawags is unfortunately my crew. So who, now, would you be?

BENJAMIN

Benjamin.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Benjamin? There must be something more to it. Benjamin Johnson? Benjamin Franklin. Benjamin Netanyahu — a personal favorite. What is it, boy? It can't be just "Benjamin."

BENJAMIN

Benjamin the Sorcerer. And I am alive.

NARRATOR

For a split second, Roberts froze in genuine surprise. Perhaps those rheumy eyes could not see well enough to detect the color of Benjamin's skin? But then the moment was over and Roberts resumed his performance, his eyes flashing wide with mock-terror as he clutched at his beard.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Did you hear that, boys? We got a wizard in our midst! A living boy. On my ship! Now that is something we don't see often.

General laughter.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

And why, o wimpy, whelpish wizard from the waking world, have you graced our dirt-drizzled decks this day? Are you lost? Looking for passage? Or are you just stupid?

BENJAMIN

I came for the seeing stone. The one you stole.

NARRATOR

A murmur ran through the crew. Captain Roberts' smile didn't waver.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Stole? That's an ugly word. I prefer claimed. I prefer took. The stone was sitting in a temple with no one watching it. Finders keepers, boy. That's the law of the sea — and the law beneath it.

BENJAMIN

It doesn't belong to you.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

It does now. But I admire your courage. Or your stupidity. Hard to tell the difference at your age. So let me give you some advice, boy, for free.

Go home. Find your little boat and row back to whatever shore you came from. The dead don't need rescuing. Your parents — oh yes, I can see it in your face, that's what this is about — your parents are dead. Do you understand what that means? It means they're done. Finished. They belong here now. What exactly do you think you're going to do, drag them back to the land of the living? Put them in their old beds and pretend nothing happened?

NARRATOR

Benjamin's hands were shaking, but he made them stop.

BENJAMIN

They don't belong here. Something took them before their time, and I'm going to find them and bring them back. I'm not leaving this ship until I get that stone.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Ah.

NARRATOR

The captain straightened up, and something flickered in those dead eyes — not quite respect, but something adjacent to it.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

A quest. How charming. How utterly pointless.

(calling)

He's made his choice, boys. Prepare the Starfish!

A general cheer goes up.

BENJAMIN

What's the Starfish?

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

I call it the Great Unburdener.

(we hear the evil grin in his voice)

Yes, the Starfish shall unburden you of liberty… limb…and life.

Roberts laughs cruelly.

The sounds of irons being clapped around Benjamin's arms and legs.

BENJAMIN

Ow! Let me go!

(beat)

I said, let me go!

PIRATE #1

Come on, now, it'll be easier for everyone if you just calmly die.

PIRATE #2

You could, of course, use a little magic. You know, brighten things up.

BENJAMIN

It doesn't work like that.

PIRATE #1

How does it work?

BENJAMIN

You wouldn't understand. What is the Starfish, anyway?

PIRATE #2

Oh, you'll see.

PIRATE #1

(helpfully, almost fondly)

Basically, we lash your hands and feet to the four winches — one for each. And then we wind 'em up, you know. Wind and wind…

PIRATE #2

Ooooh! And stretch.

PIRATE #1

Of course stretch — that's implied, you dolt. Anyway, all that stuff until you sort of… come apart.

PIRATE #2

Explode, like.

PIRATE #1

Will you please — !

(a beat)

Oh, all right. Explode. Anyway, I think you'll quite enjoy it.

If you lose, you stay. You join my crew. Forever. A living boy among the dead, serving on my ship until the stars burn out. Do we have a deal?

NARRATOR

Benjamin thought about his parents. He thought about the hooded figure on the wharf, and the seeing stone, and how far he had already come.

BENJAMIN

Deal.

INT. UNDERWORLD, PIRATE SHIP, CAPTAIN'S QUARTERS — CONTINUOUS

NARRATOR

They sat at a table in the captain's quarters, which smelled of brine and old things. The seeing stone sat on a shelf behind Roberts — a crystal orb in a knotted rope cradle, glowing faintly blue and green, like something alive.

The cards were old and soft, their faces worn almost smooth. Benjamin didn't recognize the suits — they seemed to shift when he wasn't looking directly at them — but the game itself was simple enough. Highest hand wins.

Captain Roberts dealt, his pale fingers moving with practiced ease.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Let me tell you something, boy. I've been dead a long time. Longer than you can imagine. And in all that time, do you know what I've learned?

NARRATOR

Benjamin studied his hand. Not bad. Not great.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Nothing matters. Not in the end. The living think their little lives are so important — their loves, their losses, their desperate struggles. But it all comes to the same place eventually. Here. The gray country. The endless sea.

Your parents learned that. And so will you.

BENJAMIN

My parents aren't like you.

NARRATOR

He discarded, drew again. Better.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

No? They're dead, aren't they? That makes them exactly like me.

BENJAMIN

They didn't choose to be here. You did. You chose this — the ship, the crew, the stealing. You chose to be a pirate.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

I chose freedom.

BENJAMIN

You chose to take things that don't belong to you and pretend that makes you strong. But it doesn't. It just makes you a thief.

NARRATOR

The captain looked at Benjamin's hand. Then, slowly, he looked at his own.

The silence stretched.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

(flat)

Well. It seems you've won.

NARRATOR

Benjamin reached for the stone.

A hand caught his wrist — cold and strong as iron.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

I said you won. I didn't say I was giving it to you.

NARRATOR

Benjamin tried to pull away, but the grip was unbreakable. Around him, he heard the crew moving closer, their empty eyes fixed on him.

BENJAMIN

You made a deal.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

I'm a pirate, boy.

Did you think I played fair? Did you think any of this was fair? There is no fair. There's only what you can take and what you can keep. And you — you can't keep anything.

NARRATOR

He shoved Benjamin back toward his crew.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

Besides — do you even know what this is?

They told us this little bauble was a seal. Keeping some terrible demon locked away in the deep. Some hungry old god from before the world had a name.

Well, here it sits. Here we are. And I don't see any demons, do you?

No monsters. No old gods. Just a pretty light in a rope cage. The priests and their stories — that's all it ever was. Stories to scare children.

NARRATOR

The crew was still laughing when Benjamin closed his eyes.

He reached — not for the raw force inside him, but for the stone itself. He could feel it on the shelf behind Roberts, that faint blue-green glow, and beneath the glow, something else. An impression. A memory. The shape of the thing it had been holding back.

Show me, he thought. Show me what they don't believe in.

The stone pulsed once, twice — and then the demon came.

It rose through the floor of the cabin as though the wood were water — vast and dark, its body a mass of writhing shadows and pale, bioluminescent eyes. Tentacles uncoiled across the ceiling, each one thick as a mast, lined with suckers that pulsed with cold light. A mouth opened somewhere in the darkness — no, several mouths, ringed with teeth that went back and back forever. And from those mouths came a sound: not a roar, but a whisper, a thousand whispers layered on top of each other, hungry and old and patient.

The laughter stopped.

Captain Roberts stumbled backward, his peg legs sliding on the deck. His face — his dead, gray, arrogant face — had gone pale as bone.

CAPTAIN ROBERTS

(breathing)

No. No, that's not — it can't be —

NARRATOR

The demon turned toward him. All those eyes, focusing. All those mouths, smiling.

DEMON

(whispers layered)

You took what was mine. Did you think I would not come?

NARRATOR

The crew broke. They ran — scrambling over each other, fighting to get through the door, screaming in voices that shouldn't have been able to scream. Even Roberts ran, his thunk-thunk-thunk lost in the chaos of pounding feet and howling terror.

Benjamin opened his eyes. The illusion held — he could feel it drawing on his power, a steady drain like blood from a wound — but he didn't need it to hold for long.

He grabbed the seeing stone from its shelf and ran.

EXT. UNDERWORLD, WHARF — LATER

NARRATOR

The hooded figure was waiting on the wharf, exactly where Benjamin had left him. The raven cocked its head as Benjamin approached.

HOODED FIGURE

You have it.

NARRATOR

Benjamin held out the seeing stone. It was heavier than it looked, and warm against his palms, still glowing that faint blue-green.

The figure took it with those pale, oversized hands. For a long moment, he simply held it, staring into its depths.

Then he went still.

BENJAMIN

What is it? What do you see?

HOODED FIGURE

I see… nothing.

NARRATOR

Benjamin's stomach dropped.

BENJAMIN

What do you mean, nothing?

HOODED FIGURE

I mean I see nothing. Your parents are not… visible. To the stone.

BENJAMIN

But you said —

HOODED FIGURE

I said I would look. I said I would show you where they are. But the stone cannot find them.

NARRATOR

The figure looked up, and though Benjamin still couldn't see his face, he had the sense of something shifting beneath that hood.

HOODED FIGURE

This is… unexpected.

BENJAMIN

(bitter)

Great. Thanks a lot. I risked my life for nothing.

HOODED FIGURE

No: wait!

If the stone cannot see them, that tells us something. The stone can see anywhere in the Underworld. Anywhere in the gray country, the spectral sea, the fields of the forgotten. If it cannot see your parents, then they are not in any of those places.

BENJAMIN

Then where are they?

NARRATOR

The hooded figure was silent for a long moment. The raven shifted on its post.

HOODED FIGURE

There is only one place the stone cannot reach. A place beneath this place. Deeper than the Underworld. Older.

NARRATOR

He gave the stone back to Benjamin.

HOODED FIGURE

Keep this. You will need it.

BENJAMIN

What place? Where do I have to go?

NARRATOR

The figure turned away, reaching for his guitar.

HOODED FIGURE

The Underneath. Your parents are in the Underneath.

NARRATOR

Benjamin looked out at the spectral ocean, at the distant shape of the Naglfar still burning with those sickly green lights.

The Underneath. Deeper than the Underworld.

He had a direction now. It was worse than he'd hoped.

But it was something.

He tucked the seeing stone into his coat and started walking.
